Who Were The Signers?

Famous Signers

The most important signers of The Declaration Of Independence Include:

Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence, a Founding Father, and the third President of the United States. A delegate from Virginia, he was selected for his eloquent writing style to draft the document, which was later revised by other committee members and the Continental Congress.

Benjamin Franklin
An internationally famous inventor, scientist, and diplomat, Franklin was the oldest signer at 70 years old. He was a member of the five-person committee that drafted the Declaration and made important edits to Jefferson's writing.

John Hancock
As the President of the Continental Congress, John Hancock was the first to sign the Declaration of Independence. His large, flamboyant signature is the most prominent on the document and has become a synonym for a signature.

John Adams
A delegate from Massachusetts, Adams was a key leader in the push for independence and a member of the Committee of Five that drafted the Declaration. He went on to serve as the first Vice President and second President of the United States.

Samuel Adams
A revolutionary leader from Massachusetts and cousin to John Adams, Samuel Adams was a driving force behind the independence movement. As a delegate to the Continental Congress, he signed the Declaration and later served as Governor of Massachusetts.

Benjamin Rush
A physician, chemist, and delegate from Pennsylvania, Rush was a notable signer and a well-respected figure in the colonies. He was a staunch supporter of American independence and a strong advocate for public education.

Robert Treat Paine
As a lawyer, politician, and signer of the Continental Association, Paine represented the Province of Massachusetts Bay at the signing. He later served as the state's first Attorney General and a justice of the state's Supreme Judicial Court.

These individuals were crucial due to their roles in drafting, promoting, and signing the Declaration, which laid the foundation for the United States
